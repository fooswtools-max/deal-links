Why No-Income-Tax States Get Bigger Refunds

States without a state income tax (Texas, Florida, Wyoming, Nevada) consistently have higher federal refunds. This is partly because residents in high-tax states often owe state taxes that reduce their net refund, and partly because no-income-tax states tend to have demographics that claim more refundable credits like the EITC and Child Tax Credit.

The Refund as a Savings Vehicle

Financial advisors typically recommend adjusting withholding to minimize refunds (since a refund is essentially an interest-free loan to the government). But behavioral economics research shows that many Americans use the refund as a forced savings mechanism. For low-income households in particular, the annual refund (often boosted by EITC) may be the largest single cash inflow of the year.
