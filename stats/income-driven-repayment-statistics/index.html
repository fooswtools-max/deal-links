The Negative Amortization Problem

For 57% of IDR borrowers, monthly payments don't cover the accruing interest, causing balances to grow over time. A borrower with $50,000 in debt at 6.5% accrues $271/month in interest. If their IDR payment is $132, the balance grows by $139 every month. After 10 years of payments, they may owe more than they originally borrowed. This is why IDR forgiveness after 20–25 years is structurally necessary for many borrowers.

SAVE Plan: The Biggest Change in a Generation

The SAVE plan (Saving on a Valuable Education) launched in 2023 and has already enrolled 8.1 million borrowers. It calculates payments on 5% of discretionary income for undergrad loans (vs 10% for older plans) and uses a higher income protection threshold (225% of poverty vs 150%). For a single borrower earning $35,000, SAVE payments are $0/month — compared to $105/month under the old IBR plan.
