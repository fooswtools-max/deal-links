The 1980s: When Mortgages Cost 18%

To understand today's rates, historical context is essential. In October 1981, 30-year fixed rates peaked at 18.63% as the Federal Reserve aggressively fought inflation. A $400,000 mortgage at that rate would cost $5,583/month — more than triple today's payment. Homebuyers in the early 1980s faced monthly payments that seem unimaginable today.

The 2020–2021 Anomaly

The sub-3% rates of 2020–2021 were historically unprecedented. The Fed purchased $2.7 trillion in mortgage-backed securities during the pandemic, artificially depressing rates. Those who locked in at 2.65–3.0% secured generational-low financing. This has created a "lock-in effect" where homeowners refuse to sell because moving means taking on a much higher rate.
