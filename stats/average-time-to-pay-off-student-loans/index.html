Why the "10-Year Plan" Takes 20 Years

The standard student loan repayment plan is officially 10 years. But the average borrower takes 20 years because of income-driven plan switches, deferment periods, forbearance, and consolidation resets. Each time a borrower enters deferment or switches plans, the payoff timeline extends. Only about 35% of borrowers on standard repayment actually finish in 10 years.

Medical vs Law: A Study in Contrasts

Despite having the highest debt ($215,000), medical school graduates pay off loans in 13 years — faster than any other graduate degree. High physician salaries (median $250,000+) enable aggressive repayment. Law graduates ($145,000 debt) take 19 years because legal salaries are bimodal: top-firm attorneys earn $200K+ but the median is only $80K. Many lawyers rely on PSLF through government or legal aid work.
