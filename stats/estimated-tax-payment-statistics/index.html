The Quarterly Tax Burden on Self-Employed Workers

62% of estimated tax payers are self-employed, reflecting the absence of employer withholding. These workers must set aside roughly 25–40% of each payment for income tax + self-employment tax and submit quarterly payments by April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15. Missing a deadline triggers an underpayment penalty calculated at the federal short-term rate + 3% (currently 8% annualized).

Safe Harbor Rules: How to Avoid the Penalty

The IRS provides two safe harbors to avoid underpayment penalties. Method 1: pay at least 90% of current-year tax through estimated payments and withholding. Method 2: pay at least 100% of prior-year tax (110% if AGI exceeds $150,000). Most tax advisors recommend Method 2 because it's simpler — you know exactly what you owed last year. For high-income self-employed workers with variable income, the prior-year method provides certainty.
