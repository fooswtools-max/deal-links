The FBAR Compliance Gap

Despite aggressive enforcement since FATCA (2014), FBAR compliance remains low. An estimated 9+ million Americans should file FBARs but only about 1.5 million do. The gap exists because many Americans abroad don't know about the requirement, and many domestic holders of foreign accounts (immigrants with accounts in their home country, dual citizens) are unaware that bank accounts over $10,000 in aggregate must be reported.

Penalties: The Risk of Non-Compliance

FBAR penalties are among the harshest in tax law. Non-willful violations carry a $10,000 per-account-per-year penalty. Willful violations can reach $100,000 per account or 50% of the account balance, whichever is greater. Criminal penalties include up to 5 years in prison. The IRS Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedures offer a way to come into compliance with reduced penalties for those who were non-willful.
