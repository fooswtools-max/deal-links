Why State Differences Are So Large

Student debt varies enormously by state because of differences in public university tuition, state grant programs, and enrollment patterns. Northeastern states like Connecticut and New Hampshire have higher private college enrollment and less state aid, leading to higher debt. Western states like Utah have lower tuition at public universities and higher community college usage, keeping debt down.

The $1.77 Trillion Picture

Total outstanding student loan debt has reached $1.77 trillion, held by 43.5 million borrowers. The average balance per borrower is $37,850 for undergraduate debt, but including graduate and professional school debt, the average rises to $40,700. About 7% of borrowers owe $100,000 or more, accounting for roughly one-third of all outstanding debt — primarily from graduate and professional programs (law, medicine, MBA).
