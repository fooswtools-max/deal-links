The Unique US-Israel Tax Relationship

The US is one of only two countries (along with Eritrea) that taxes citizens on worldwide income regardless of where they live. This means all 210,000+ American expats in Israel are legally required to file US tax returns. However, most owe nothing: the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion ($126,500 in 2026) and the Foreign Tax Credit (for Israeli taxes paid) typically eliminate US tax liability since Israeli tax rates are generally higher than US rates.

The Compliance Gap in Israel

Despite owing little to no US tax, only 55% of US expats in Israel file returns, and only 25% file the required FBAR for foreign bank accounts. Many expats don't file because they don't realize they're still required to, or because they believe (incorrectly) that owing no tax means no filing obligation. This creates significant risk: unfiled returns carry penalties, and unfiled FBARs can result in $10,000+ per-account penalties.
