When Refinancing Makes Sense (and When It Doesn't)

Refinancing makes sense if you have strong credit (700+), stable income, no plans to use IDR or PSLF, and current rates above your refinance quote. It does NOT make sense if you work in public service (PSLF eligible), have unstable income (need IDR as a safety net), or if your federal rate is already below available private rates. The savings can be enormous — $21,000 on average — but losing federal protections is permanent.

The Rate Environment in 2026

Refinancing rates have stabilized after the sharp increases of 2022–2024. With the 10-year Treasury around 4.4%, the best fixed refinancing rates are in the 5.2–5.5% range for top-credit borrowers. This is well below the current federal graduate rate of 8.08% and PLUS rate of 9.08%, making refinancing particularly attractive for grad school debt. Undergraduate borrowers with 6.53% federal rates see smaller savings.
