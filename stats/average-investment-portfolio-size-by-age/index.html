The Wealth Gap in Investment Portfolios

The gap between average and median portfolio sizes reveals dramatic inequality in investment ownership. For Americans aged 45–54, the average portfolio ($521,000) is nearly 3x the median ($186,000). This skew is driven by the concentration of financial assets among high earners — the top 10% of households own approximately 70% of all stock market wealth.

How Age Affects Asset Allocation

Portfolio composition shifts significantly with age. Investors under 35 typically hold 85–90% stocks, while those over 65 hold 45–55% stocks. This shift toward bonds and cash is a natural de-risking strategy as investors approach and enter retirement. The traditional rule of thumb (hold your age in bonds) has evolved, with most financial advisors now recommending a more stock-heavy allocation given longer life expectancies.
