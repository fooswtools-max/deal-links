Why Median Net Worth Matters More

The average American family has a net worth of over $1 million — but that number is wildly misleading. A handful of ultra-wealthy families pull the average way up. The median ($192,900) is a much better indicator of the typical family's financial position. If you have a net worth above the median for your age group, you're doing better than half of Americans.

The Homeownership Effect

The single biggest driver of middle-class wealth is homeownership. Homeowners have a median net worth of $396,200 versus just $10,400 for renters. This is because a home is a leveraged, appreciating asset with forced savings (monthly mortgage payments build equity). Over 30 years, this compounding effect creates enormous wealth differences between owners and renters.
