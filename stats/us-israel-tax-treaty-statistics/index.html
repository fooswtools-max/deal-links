How the Treaty Eliminates Double Taxation

The US-Israel tax treaty works primarily through the credit method: US citizens in Israel pay Israeli tax on their worldwide income (Israeli rates are generally 10–50%), then claim a Foreign Tax Credit on their US return for the Israeli taxes paid. Since Israeli rates are generally higher than US rates at comparable income levels, the FTC typically eliminates all US tax liability. Combined with the FEIE ($126,500 exclusion in 2026), most expats owe nothing to the IRS.

Pension and Social Security: The Totalization Agreement

The US-Israel Social Security Totalization Agreement (in effect since 1995) prevents dual social security taxation and allows workers to combine credits from both countries to qualify for benefits. A worker who spent 5 years in the US and 15 years in Israel can use both work records to qualify for US Social Security. Israeli Bituach Leumi contributions are creditable against US self-employment tax for self-employed expats.
