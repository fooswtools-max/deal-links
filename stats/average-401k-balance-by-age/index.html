Why Median Matters More Than Average

The average 401(k) balance is nearly 4x the median, which tells us that a small number of people with very large balances pull the average up significantly. The median ($35,286) is a more realistic picture of what the typical worker has saved. If your balance is above the median for your age group, you’re doing better than half of all workers.

The Employer Match: Free Money Most People Leave on the Table

About 1 in 5 employees don't contribute enough to get their full employer match. With the average match at 4.7%, an employee earning $60,000 who doesn't max their match is leaving $2,820 per year on the table. Over a 30-year career with 7% growth, that missed match costs over $285,000 in retirement savings.

How Long-Tenured Employees Build Wealth

Tenure matters enormously for 401(k) balances. Workers with 10+ years at their company average $306,800 versus $48,200 for those with less than 2 years. This reflects both consistent contributions and compound growth. The message is clear: start early, contribute consistently, and let time do the heavy lifting.
