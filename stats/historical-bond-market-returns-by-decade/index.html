The 40-Year Bond Bull Market (1981–2020)

From 1981 to 2020, bonds enjoyed a historic bull run as interest rates fell from 18% to near 0%. Falling rates boost bond prices, so investors earned both coupon income and capital appreciation. The 1980s delivered 12.4% annual returns from the Aggregate Bond Index — equity-like returns with lower volatility. This era conditioned an entire generation to view bonds as reliable, high-returning assets.

The 2022 Bond Crash: A Wake-Up Call

2022 shattered the myth that bonds are always safe. The Bloomberg US Aggregate Bond Index fell 13.0% — its worst year since the index began in 1976. Long-term Treasuries fell over 30%. The culprit: the Fed's aggressive rate hikes from near-zero to 5.25%. For the traditional 60/40 portfolio, 2022 was a historically bad year because stocks and bonds fell simultaneously.
