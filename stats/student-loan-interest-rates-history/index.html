How Federal Student Loan Rates Are Set

Since 2013, federal student loan rates have been tied to the 10-year Treasury yield plus a fixed margin: +2.05% for undergrad, +3.60% for graduate, and +4.60% for PLUS loans. The rate is set each May based on the Treasury auction and takes effect July 1. This means rates rise when the broader economy pushes Treasury yields higher — exactly what happened in 2022–2025.

The Golden Era: 2020–2021

The 2020–21 academic year saw the lowest federal student loan rates in history: 2.75% for undergraduates. The 10-year Treasury fell to 0.66% during the pandemic, driving rates to rock bottom. Borrowers who took out loans that year locked in historically cheap federal financing. Since then, rates have more than doubled as the Fed fought inflation.
