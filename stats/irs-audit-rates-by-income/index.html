Key Statistics at a Glance

  • The overall audit rate is 0.38% (roughly 1 in 260 returns)
  • Filers earning $1M+ have a 2.4% audit rate — 6x higher than average
  • EITC claimants are audited at 1.1% — nearly 3x the overall rate
  • IRS audit rates have declined 60% since 2010 due to budget cuts
  • Most audits (75%) are conducted by correspondence (mail), not in person

The EITC Audit Controversy

Low-income filers claiming the Earned Income Tax Credit are audited at 1.1% — a rate comparable to millionaires. This has drawn Congressional criticism because EITC audits are low-cost correspondence audits (sent by mail), while audits of high-income taxpayers require expensive in-person examinations. The result: the IRS chooses volume over value, auditing many low-income filers while understaffing complex high-income audits.

IRS Funding and the Audit Decline

Audit rates have collapsed since 2010, when the overall rate was 1.1% (vs 0.38% today). IRS staffing fell from 94,000 employees in 2010 to 79,000 in 2022 as Congress cut the agency's budget by 20% in real terms. The Inflation Reduction Act (2022) provided $80 billion in new IRS funding, with $45 billion specifically for enforcement. The IRS plans to increase audit rates for filers above $400,000 while maintaining or reducing rates for those below.
