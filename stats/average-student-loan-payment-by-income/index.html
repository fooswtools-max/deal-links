The Regressive Burden of Student Debt

Student loan payments are deeply regressive. Low-income borrowers don't just pay a higher share of income — they often carry similar debt levels to higher earners (since tuition doesn't scale with income). A borrower earning $24,000 paying $185/month is spending 18% of gross income, leaving little for housing, food, and other basics. This is why income-driven repayment plans are so critical for low-income borrowers.

Income-Driven Repayment: The Safety Net

62% of borrowers earning under $25,000 are enrolled in income-driven repayment plans, which cap payments at 5–20% of discretionary income. Under the SAVE plan (introduced 2023), many low-income borrowers have $0 monthly payments. But enrollment drops sharply with income: only 8% of borrowers earning $150K+ use IDR, preferring to pay off loans faster on standard plans.
