What are the US tax and FBAR rules for Americans living in Brazil?

Americans in Brazil must file a US return on worldwide income and an FBAR when foreign accounts top $10,000 aggregate at any point in the year. There is no comprehensive US-Brazil income tax treaty, so the Foreign Tax Credit is the main tool to avoid double taxation — Brazilian tax paid offsets US liability. The FEIE can exclude up to ~$126,500 of earned income for 2024.

Common questions

No treaty — does that mean double tax?

Not usually. The Foreign Tax Credit still applies even without a treaty, crediting Brazilian tax against US tax.

Are Brazilian investments a problem?

Foreign funds can be PFICs with heavy US reporting. Review holdings with a cross-border specialist.
