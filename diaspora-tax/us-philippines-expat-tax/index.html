How do US citizens in the Philippines handle US taxes and FBAR?

US citizens in the Philippines file a US return on worldwide income plus an FBAR if foreign accounts exceed $10,000 aggregate during the year. The Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (~$126,500 for 2024) and Foreign Tax Credit usually eliminate double taxation. The US-Philippines treaty governs which country taxes specific income types.

Common questions

Is Philippine 13th-month pay US-taxable?

Yes — it's earned income, reportable to the IRS, though it may fall under the FEIE.

Do I file if I owe nothing?

Yes. Filing is required above the threshold even when credits and exclusions zero out the tax.
