Do US citizens living in India need to file FBAR and US taxes?

Yes. US citizens in India must file a US tax return on worldwide income and an FBAR (FinCEN Form 114) if their foreign accounts exceed $10,000 aggregate at any point in the year. The US-India tax treaty and the Foreign Tax Credit prevent most double taxation — Indian tax paid offsets US liability. The Foreign Earned Income Exclusion can exclude up to ~$126,500 of earned income for 2024.

Common questions

Does the US-India treaty stop double taxation?

Largely, via the Foreign Tax Credit — tax paid in India offsets US tax owed. Some income types still need care.

What about Indian PPF or mutual funds?

Indian mutual funds are often PFICs with complex US reporting (Form 8621). PPF interest is US-taxable. Get specialist advice.
