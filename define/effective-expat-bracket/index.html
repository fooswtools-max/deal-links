Effective Expat Bracket

Effective Expat Bracket is the true marginal tax rate a US citizen abroad pays after applying the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion and Foreign Tax Credit — often far below the nominal US bracket, sometimes zero.

Formula

effective_bracket = (US_tax_owed - FTC - FEIE_benefit) / taxable_income

Worked example

A US expat in Israel earning $120,000 may have a nominal 24% bracket but an effective expat bracket near 0% once the FEIE and treaty credits apply.

Why it matters

The effective expat bracket turns a fuzzy intuition into a single comparable number, so you can make the decision on math instead of vibes.
