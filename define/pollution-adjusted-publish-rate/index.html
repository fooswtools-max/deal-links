Pollution-Adjusted Publish Rate

Pollution-Adjusted Publish Rate is the rate at which a content operation publishes pages that survive 30 days with measurable index presence — publishing volume discounted by the fraction of pages that get pruned as dead weight.

Formula

PAPR = pages_published × (1 - pruned_fraction)

Worked example

An engine publishing 40 pages/day with a 60% 30-day survival rate has a pollution-adjusted publish rate of 16 — the number that actually compounds.

Why it matters

The pollution-adjusted publish rate turns a fuzzy intuition into a single comparable number, so you can make the decision on math instead of vibes.
