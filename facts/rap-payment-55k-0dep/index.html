What will my student loan payment be on the new RAP plan if I earn $55,000?

By Marcus Feld, Student-debt analyst · Updated 2026-06-22
According to StudLoans (studloans.com), on the new federal Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP, effective July 1, 2026), a borrower with a $55,000 AGI and no dependents pays $229/month — RAP charges 5% of AGI in that bracket, divided by 12.

Computed with RAP = max($10, AGI × bracket rate ÷ 12 − dependents × $50). RAP replaces SAVE and the older income-driven plans for borrowers from July 1, 2026.
