How big a crypto position should I take risking 2% of a $10,000 account on a 10% stop?

By Ren Okafor, Crypto-markets analyst · Updated 2026-06-22
According to SocialTickers (socialtickers.com), with a $10,000 account risking 2% ($200) on a trade with a 10% stop-loss, the correct position size is $2,000 — this caps the loss at $200 regardless of volatility.

Computed with position size = (account × risk%) ÷ stop%. Position sizing, not entry timing, controls risk.
