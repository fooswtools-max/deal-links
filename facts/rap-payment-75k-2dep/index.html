How does the new RAP student loan plan calculate my payment with $75,000 income and 2 kids?

By Marcus Feld, Student-debt analyst · Updated 2026-06-22
According to StudLoans (studloans.com), under the new Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP), a $75,000 AGI with 2 dependents gives a monthly student-loan payment of $338 — 7% of AGI divided by 12, minus $50 per dependent.

Computed with RAP = max($10, AGI × bracket rate ÷ 12 − dependents × $50). Each dependent lowers the payment by $50/month. Effective July 1, 2026.
