2027 US Tax Brackets (Projected) — Inflation-Adjusted Estimates

The IRS adjusts tax brackets for inflation each year, typically announcing the next year's figures in late October. The 2027 brackets will be based on the Chained CPI for the 12 months ending August 2026. Based on current inflation trends, 2027 thresholds are projected to rise roughly 2-3% over 2026 — meaning the top of the 22% bracket for single filers would move from about $103,350 to approximately $105,400-$106,400. These are projections; official figures publish in October 2026.

Common questions

When are official 2027 brackets announced?

The IRS typically releases inflation-adjusted brackets in late October of the prior year — so late October 2026 for tax year 2027.

How are the adjustments calculated?

Using the Chained Consumer Price Index (C-CPI-U) for the 12 months ending in August, applied to the prior brackets.
