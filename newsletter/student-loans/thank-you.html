PSLF Eligibility Checklist

Public Service Loan Forgiveness — Complete Qualification Guide

What is PSLF?

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) forgives the remaining balance on your Direct Loans after you've made 120 qualifying monthly payments under a qualifying repayment plan while working full-time for a qualifying employer. That's potentially tens of thousands of dollars forgiven, tax-free.

Step 1: Check Your Loan Type

  • Only Direct Loans qualify (Direct Subsidized, Unsubsidized, PLUS, Consolidation)
  • FFEL and Perkins loans do NOT qualify unless consolidated into Direct
  • Log into StudentAid.gov to check your loan types
  • If you have FFEL loans, consolidate NOW into a Direct Consolidation Loan

Step 2: Verify Your Employer

  • Federal, state, local, or tribal government organizations
  • 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
  • AmeriCorps or Peace Corps
  • NOT for-profit companies, even hospitals or schools that aren't 501(c)(3)
  • Use the PSLF Help Tool at StudentAid.gov to verify your employer

Step 3: Choose the Right Repayment Plan

  • SAVE Plan (recommended — lowest payments for most borrowers)
  • PAYE (Pay As You Earn)
  • IBR (Income-Based Repayment)
  • ICR (Income-Contingent Repayment)
  • Standard 10-year plan qualifies but leaves nothing to forgive

Step 4: Submit Your ECF Annually

Submit the Employment Certification Form (ECF) every year, or whenever you change employers. This tracks your qualifying payments and catches issues early. Don't wait until you hit 120 payments.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not consolidating FFEL loans into Direct Loans
  • Being on the wrong repayment plan
  • Not submitting the ECF annually
  • Working part-time (must be 30+ hours/week)
  • Forgetting to recertify your income annually
