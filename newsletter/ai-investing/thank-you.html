Top 10 Index Funds for 2026

Data-Backed Picks for Long-Term Wealth Building

Why Index Funds in 2026?

Index funds remain the single best investment vehicle for most people. With expense ratios as low as 0.03%, you keep more of your returns. In 2026, with market volatility and AI disruption reshaping sectors, choosing the right index funds matters more than ever.

Our Selection Criteria

  • Expense ratio under 0.10%
  • Minimum 5-year track record
  • AUM over $10 billion for liquidity
  • Broad diversification across sectors
  • Tax efficiency for taxable accounts

The Top 10 Index Funds

#1

Vanguard Total Stock Market (VTI/VTSAX)

Entire US market in one fund. 0.03% expense ratio. The cornerstone of any portfolio.

#2

Vanguard S&P 500 (VOO/VFIAX)

500 largest US companies. 0.03% expense ratio. Warren Buffett's recommendation.

#3

Vanguard Total International (VXUS/VTIAX)

Global diversification outside US. Essential for reducing country risk.

#4

Schwab US Broad Market (SCHB)

Ultra-low 0.03% ratio. Great Vanguard alternative with no minimums.

#5

Fidelity ZERO Total Market (FZROX)

0.00% expense ratio. Yes, zero. Perfect for Fidelity accounts.

#6

Vanguard Total Bond Market (BND/VBTLX)

Core bond allocation. Stabilizes portfolio during stock downturns.

#7

iShares Core S&P Mid-Cap (IJH)

Mid-cap exposure for higher growth potential. 0.05% expense ratio.

#8

Vanguard Real Estate (VNQ)

Real estate exposure without owning property. Dividend income focus.

#9

Vanguard Growth ETF (VUG)

Tilt toward growth stocks. Heavier tech/innovation weighting.

#10

iShares Core MSCI Emerging Markets (IEMG)

Emerging market growth. Higher risk, higher potential returns.

How to Build Your Portfolio

A simple 3-fund portfolio works for most investors: 60% VTI (US stocks), 30% VXUS (international), 10% BND (bonds). Adjust based on your age and risk tolerance. Younger investors can go 80/20 stocks/bonds; those near retirement might prefer 50/50.
