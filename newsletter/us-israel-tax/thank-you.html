FBAR Filing Guide for Israeli Expats

FinCEN Form 114 — Foreign Bank Account Reporting

What is FBAR?

FBAR (Foreign Bank Account Report) is a mandatory annual filing for US citizens and residents who have a financial interest in or signature authority over foreign financial accounts if the aggregate value exceeds $10,000 at any time during the year. For Americans in Israel, this almost always applies.

Who Must File?

  • US citizens living in Israel (dual citizens included)
  • US green card holders residing in Israel
  • Anyone with Israeli bank accounts totaling $10,000+ at any point in the year
  • This includes checking, savings, brokerage, pension (keren pensia), and bituach menahalim accounts

What Accounts to Report

  • Israeli bank accounts (Bank Leumi, Hapoalim, Discount, Mizrahi, etc.)
  • Kupat Gemel (provident funds)
  • Keren Pensia (pension funds)
  • Bituach Menahalim (insurance-manager funds)
  • Keren Hishtalmut (continuing education funds)
  • Brokerage accounts at Israeli institutions
  • Joint accounts where you have signature authority

Filing Deadlines

  • Due April 15 (same as tax return)
  • Automatic extension to October 15
  • Additional extension to December 15 available upon request
  • File electronically via FinCEN BSA E-Filing System

Penalties for Non-Filing

  • Non-willful: Up to $10,000 per violation
  • Willful: Up to $100,000 or 50% of account balance (whichever is greater)
  • Criminal penalties possible for willful violations
  • Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedures available for those catching up

How to File: Step by Step

  • Gather maximum account balances for each foreign account during the year
  • Convert ILS to USD using Treasury Department year-end exchange rate
  • Go to FinCEN BSA E-Filing System (bsaefiling.fincen.treas.gov)
  • Complete FinCEN Form 114 online
  • Submit electronically — paper filing is NOT accepted
  • Save your confirmation for your records

FBAR vs. FATCA (Form 8938)

FBAR and FATCA are separate requirements. FBAR goes to FinCEN (threshold: $10,000). FATCA Form 8938 goes to the IRS with your tax return (threshold: $200,000 for expats filing single, $400,000 for married filing jointly). Most Israeli expats must file both.
