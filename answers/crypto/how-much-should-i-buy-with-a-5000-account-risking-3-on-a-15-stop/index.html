How much should I buy with a $5000 account risking 3% on a 15% stop?

With a $5,000 account risking 3% ($150) on a trade with a 15% stop-loss, your position size should be $1,000. This caps your loss at $150 if the stop triggers, regardless of how volatile the asset is. Position sizing — not entry timing — is what keeps traders solvent.

How this is calculated

Position size = (account × risk%) ÷ stop%. This caps dollar loss at your chosen risk level regardless of asset volatility.
